## 👋 Hey

I'm Philippe — or **Kippie**, if you know me.

I'm from **Knokke-Heist**, a small coastal town in West Flanders, Belgium, right where the North Sea meets the Dutch border. I build software for a living through my company Reviewell BV — but this page has nothing to do with that.

This is the non-pitch version. No products, no tech stack badges, no "open to opportunities." Just who I am when I'm not staring at a screen.

## 📍 Where I'm From

```
Country:    Belgium 🇧🇪
Region:     West Flanders (West-Vlaanderen)
City:       Knokke-Heist
Coast:      North Sea
Population: ~35,000
Vibe:       Small, quiet, close to the water — and I wouldn't trade it
```

Knokke-Heist is a coastal town that most Belgians know for summer. I know it year-round. The off-season is when it's actually good — empty beaches, wind on the water, and a pace of life that makes sense.

## 🪁 Kitesurfing

The North Sea is right there. On the right day — the right wind direction, the right tide — there is genuinely nowhere better to be.

```
Spot:         Belgian/Dutch North Sea coast
Conditions:   Side-onshore, 15-25 knots, clean water
Wind season:  Autumn and spring are best
The appeal:   It fully switches off the brain.
              You cannot think about code while kitesurfing.
              That is the point.
```

It's one of the few activities where I'm genuinely not thinking about anything else. No product roadmap, no bugs, no ideas. Just wind and water.

## ♟️ Chess

I play chess online and occasionally over the board. It sits in an interesting place mentally — everything is visible, nothing is hidden, every mistake is your own fault, and no one can take it from you.

```
Format:       Blitz and rapid online, classical OTB when possible
Style:        Positional — I like building structures, not gambling
What I like:  The honesty of it. You can't blame circumstances.
```

It's a different kind of thinking than software. In software there's always ambiguity, external dependencies, things you can't control. Chess is just you and the board.

## 🧠 How I Think

I'm not someone who waits for permission or the right conditions. If I want to understand how something works, I figure it out. If I think something should exist, I try to build it.

```
Learning style:   Doing first, understanding why second
Problem solving:  Find the real cause, not the surface symptom
Decision making:  Move fast on reversible decisions, careful on big ones
On failure:       It's data. Fix it and move forward.
On complexity:    Most things are simpler than they look — and a few things
                  are more complex than anyone admits upfront.
```

I don't have formal training in software, business, or anything else I do professionally. That has costs — slower at the start, lots of dead ends — and benefits: no theoretical constraints, just real-world feedback loops.
